The Viking Spirit: An Introduction to Norse Mythology and Religion

by Daniel McCoy

Discover the myths, gods, and spiritual beliefs that defined the Viking age — presented with clarity, depth, and storytelling flair.

The Viking Spirit is the ultimate modern guide to Norse mythology and religion, offering readers a powerful mix of historical insight, mythological retellings, and cultural commentary. Written by Daniel McCoy — the mind behind the popular site Norse Mythology for Smart People — this book blends rigorous scholarship with an easy-to-read narrative voice that makes complex ideas feel both accessible and engaging.

Inside, you’ll find 34 vividly retold Norse myths, including tales of Odin’s sacrifice on the world tree, Thor’s fierce battles with the giants, Loki’s schemes, and the tragic destiny of Ragnarök. But The Viking Spirit goes far beyond myth summaries. McCoy guides readers through the Viking worldview — explaining how Norse religion functioned, what Vikings believed about fate, death, the soul, magic, honor, and the sacred forces behind natural phenomena.

Drawing from the latest research in Norse studies, McCoy connects myth with meaning — showing not just what the Vikings believed, but why those beliefs mattered in everyday life, politics, warfare, and ritual. The result is a rare combination: a book that is both enjoyable for casual readers and valuable for students, writers, and researchers.
